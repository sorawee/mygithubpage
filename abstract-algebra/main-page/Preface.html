1 Preface

I enrolled in a course on Abstract Algebra in Fall 2015 and did not like it much. We used Dummit and Foote’s Abstract Algebra which I hated because the book is super dense, giving me a bad impression at first glance. It also does not give a good motivation to study the content. In particular, I was under the impression that I needed to memorize all terms, and Dummit and Foote’s introduces a bunch at will without a convincing reason why they are important.

After one semester, I re-read the book along with Charles C. Pinter’s A Book of Abstract Algebra. It’s this time that I started to fully understand some content that I didn’t really understand before. To motivate myself further to read the books, I decided to write this book, so that I need to thoroughly understand the content first in order to write it.

This book is written using a modified Soegaard’s #lang mathbook, an extension of Scribble which is a domain-specific language for writing documentations and books. One of the reasons I write this book is that I believe that an electronic book is more than a book with hyperlinks, as it can interact with readers. Therefore, this book is an experiment which integrates this belief when writing. It is also an experiment to explore what Scribble could do.
